Family-Friendly Winery Bus Tours in Adelaide: Yes, It’s Possible!

Parents, we hear you. How many times have you had to cut your trip short from exploring the best regional wines or visiting cellar doors because the children are “bored?” Well, here’s the good news. A day at a local vineyard or winery in Adelaide isn’t exclusive to adults anymore. Yes, you read that right: there are many family-friendly wineries in Adelaide that you can add to your list the next time you embark on a winery bus tour. It’s the ultimate win-win as you sample the best local wines and children enjoy plenty of entertainment. Before you book your next adventure with an Adelaide bus tour, make a note of these family-friendly wineries.

Top 8 Family-Friendly Wineries in Adelaide

1. Sidewood Cellar Door and Restaurant in Hahndorf

Located in Adelaide Hills, this cellar door and restaurant is a family-friendly destination you should add as a stop on your Adelaide bus tour. It proves kids can have as much fun as adults in a vineyard! Visiting here will have your kids mingling with other kids and families.

Aside from its exceptional wine selection, kids can explore their nature playground. There are deck chairs and daybeds where you can relax or enjoy a picnic with the family, featuring your wine haul from the cellar door. There is also a restaurant if you want to be treated to exquisite food and wine pairings.

The adults can enjoy the picturesque landscape and wines while the kids have all the fun they can get from climbing, swinging, and jumping in the playground.

2. The Vine Shed in McLaren Vale

The Vine Shed is another family-friendly winery in McLaren Vale, within the picturesque Fleurieu Peninsula. It is a popular cellar door in McLaren Vale, but there is more to it than meets the eye. It is a family-friendly restaurant, wedding venue, and live music venue. The aesthetic of the cellar door blends rustic and modern décor elements.

Aside from its vast dining space, a bridge outdoors leads to a small rocky waterfall. It’s an excellent opportunity to take the kids on a tiny excursion. There is also a large lawn where kids can run around or play. This area is equipped with outdoor games, swings, and a cubby. There is also a small pond with fish and ducks for outdoor-loving kids. They would enjoy the opportunity to interact with these animals.

3. Barrister’s Block in Woodside, Adelaide Hills

Adding to the list of must-visit spots for wine-loving families going on a winery bus tour in Adelaide is the Barrister’s Block. This winery boasts acres of manicured gardens that the kids can explore while you sip your favourite wines and enjoy a tasting of your picnic platter. You can roll out a picnic blanket while the kids run around or sit beside the massive sandpit. There are farm animals that your little ones will be thrilled to interact with.

4. Whistler Wines in Barossa Valley

File Whistler Wines under your must-visit wineries for family as part of your Barossa Valley wine tour. This winery sits in a dreamy location: imagine rolling hills and lush landscapes. It is a hidden gem for families looking to indulge their wine-craving palate.

Aside from their exquisite wine selection, they have impressive artisan cheese and cured meats. It is an excellent destination for foodies who want to sample the best of the Barossa Valley’s culinary tradition. Their food selection for kids is equally impressive, too. They will enjoy interacting with the wildlife and rescue kangaroos on-site.

5. Down the Rabbit Hole

For a winery with such a name, it’s an intriguing destination for wine-loving families embarking on a winery bus tour in Adelaide. If you add this as a stop, you won’t be disappointed!

This winery sits between a vineyard and an orchard, offering an idyllic setting for families to enjoy a relaxing picnic under the afternoon sun. Meanwhile, the kids can run around or kick a ball on the lush lawns. There are animals, such as alpacas and chickens, for them to play with.

When you visit Down the Rabbit Hole, don’t forget to take a photo at the famous vintage double-decker bus as a souvenir!

6. Woodstock Wine Estate in McLaren Flat

Woodstock Wine Estate is another go-to destination on your winery bus tour. Because of its own wildlife park, kids will enjoy it as much as adults!

Indeed, kids can explore the gardens within the property and check out the animal farm. They can meet or feed the ponies, alpacas, goats, and wallabies. When they're done feeding the animals, a children’s playground is also available.

This expansive wine estate is the perfect place for kids to burn their energy while adults sip their favourite wines and local cuisine.

7. Fork in the Road Wines in McLaren Vale

Also known as the Old Oval Estate, Fork in the Road Wines is a family-friendly winery where kids won’t get bored while you enjoy wine tasting. There are plenty of spaces for kids to explore during your stay.

First, gorgeous manicured gardens and a beautiful patio area for families exist. The garden has a massive tree where kids can play and a giant sandpit for more exploration. It is also a great spot for parents to sit while watching their kids play, especially as it overlooks the vines.

The food platter is kid-friendly, so the kids won’t mind sharing the food!

8. Home of Plenty in Currency Creek

If your Adelaide bus tour takes you to the Currency Creek area, stop by Home of Plenty. This winery is a dream for families with smaller kids. Its cellar door offers the best wines and lots of green, lush space for the kids to run free.

The laid back atmosphere is perfect for soaking in the natural beauty and lush landscape. Since it is far from the main road, you aren’t disturbed by the noise of cars passing by. Take this opportunity to relax and sip your favourite local wines while the kids have the time of their lives!

Grab a picnic rug and savour your food and wine pairings in a beautiful setting if you want to make it extra cosy.
